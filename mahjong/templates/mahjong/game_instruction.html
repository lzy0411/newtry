Objective of the Game

Mahjong is a four-player chess game. The objective of the game is to collect tiles to form a Mahjong hand. Players take turns picking one tile from the tile set and discarding one tile. When for example player A picks up a tile that gives a valid Mahjong hand or the other player discards a tile that player A can claim to form a valid Mahjong hand, then player A wins.


Tiles

A full Mahjong tile set contains 136 tiles. There are three suit tiles: Bamboo, Characters and Dots. Each suit has tiles numbered one to nine. There is four of each tile. There are also honor tiles: dragons and winds. The dragons are named Red, White and Green. The winds are East, South, West and North. There is four of each dragon type and four of each wind in the tile set.



Mahjong Hands

A Mahjong hand normally consists of 4 good sets and 1 pair of the same tiles. A set is good if there are three ordering tiles of the same class or totally three same tiles.


Here is a typical example of a winning set:
  • 1st good set: [Dots1, Dots2, Dots3]
  • 2nd good set: [Bamboo5, Bamboo6, Bamboo7]
  • 3rd good set: [Dots6, Dots7, Dots8]
  • 4th good set: [East, East, East]
  • 1 Pair: [Bamboo 9] [Bamboo 9]


Game Flow

Players start with 13 tiles each. Player1 starts the game by picking a tile from the tile set. Players then take counterclockwise turns picking a tile from the tile set and then discarding one tile from the hand. It is also possible to claim a tile discarded by another player under certain circumstances (described below). Players shall always have a total of 13 tiles in hand, until Mahjong is declared. (A Mahjong hand contains 14 tiles.)


Claiming Tiles

  • A player may claim a discarded tile from last player to declare a PONG (the same three tiles, for example, Dots1, Dots1, Dots1).
  • A player may claim a discarded tile from last player to declare an EAT (three ordering tiles of the same class, for example player A has Dots6 and Dots8, then last player discards Dots7 then player A can claim Dots7 to form a good set with Dots6, Dots7, Dots8).

When claiming a discarded tile, the picked tile in this round is automatically discarded

By this rule, a player may claim a discarded tile from last player by EAT OR PONG to form a Mahjong hand and win.
Tip: it is not always wise to claim discarded tiles by Pong as players need a pair to form Mahjong hand.


Tripe and Eat Tiles

  • A player may recollect the recent discarded tile from the last player to declare a TRIPLE (the same three tiles, for example, Dots1, Dots1, Dots1).
  • A player may recollect the recent discarded tile from the last player to declare an EAT (three ordering tiles of the same class, for example player A has Dots6 and Dots8, then last player discards Dots7 then player A can claim Dots7 to form a good set with Dots6, Dots7, Dots8).
A Tip: It is suggested that when you are going to win, do not to leave a single tile in hand, which is not within any good sets, since it is impossible to triple or eat a tile from the last player to make a pair.
